Climate Change:

Climate change isthe change in global or regional climate patterns attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.
Majority do not pay mind to the matter of climate change as they should and as time progresses it continues to become a larger problem if no action is taken. Humans continue to rely on the use of nonrenewable energy sources and portray a careless attitude towards the progressing conflict of climate change. Carbon pollution from burning fossil fuels like coal, oil and natural gas is warming our planet and throwing natural systems out of balance and is also at an all time high from the fossil fuel industry.
Climate change is witnessed throughout the lives of people. And it is up to the people to take action to combat it. To preserve our planet and further advance its stability. Everything humans have been doing to hurt the environment needs to be altered to reduce the decay of our planet.

Natural Disasters:

Climate change has dramatically shifted to portray extreme weather causing natural disasters.
Irresponsible human activity has caused climate change to intensify and our own actions have been disrupting wildlife and nature itself.

The Exxon Valdez Oil Spill:



The Exxon Valdez Oil spill was one of the most environmentally damaging oil spills in world history.
Although there were no human casualties,
302 seals
2,800 Otters
And a remarkable number of birds were killed.
Even after 30 years, animal populations have not recovered.



Hurricane Florence and Hurricane Michael:

Michael
Florence











Hurricane Michael was the strongest storm to ever hit Florida panhandle,
Killing over 40 people
With 155 mph winds (Just 2 mph short from a category 5)
Major damage also reported in Mexico beach as well as Panama City.
The southeastern U.S was widely affected by Michael.


Hurricane Florence brought disastrous flooding to the carolinas
Killing over 50 people
Causing billions of dollars of property damage
While surpassing all time rainfall and flood records in both states.

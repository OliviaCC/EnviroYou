Air Pollution


Air pollution is a mixture of solid particles and gases in the air that we breath, and it is typically separated into two categories, outdoor and indoor air pollution.

Outdoor air pollution

Involves exposures that occur outside of a built environment, the mixing of harmful particles and gases. This can trigger asthma episodes that can sometimes shape how kids lungs develop.

Indoor air pollution

Involves exposures to pollutants like carbon dioxide, carried by air indoors and/or dust. An example of this includes household cleaning objects or mold.

Health effects

In the past 30 years, researchers have found a large array of health effects, including but not limited to respiratory diseases, pregnancy issues, and death. In 2013, it was discovered that outdoor air pollution is cancer fuel.
